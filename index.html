CODE STEMI
ST-Elevation Myocardial Infarction — Emergency Management Protocol
🚨
STEMI = Time-Critical Emergency. Every minute of delay = ~2 million cardiomyocytes lost. Goal: D2B ≤90 min (PCI) or D2N ≤30 min (fibrinolysis).

🫀 Pathophysiology in Brief
Plaque rupture → platelet activation → thrombus → complete coronary occlusion → transmural ischemia → ST elevation → cell death begins within 20 min (subendocardium → epicardium). The "wave-front" of necrosis is time-dependent — time is myocardium.

⚠️ STEMI Mimics — Don't Be Fooled
🫀 LBBB + STEMI (Sgarbossa Criteria)▼
🔑
Modified Sgarbossa (≥3 points = STEMI-equivalent):
CriterionPoints
ST↑ ≥1mm concordant with QRS (same direction)+5
ST↓ ≥1mm in V1, V2, or V3 (concordant)+3
ST↑ ≥5mm discordant (opposite to QRS) — Smith modification: ≥25% of S-wave+2
💡
In LBBB: discordant ST changes are expected (opposite to main QRS deflection). CONCORDANT changes = ischemia!

🔎 STEMI Equivalents (No Classic ST↑)▼
🚨
These patterns = complete coronary occlusion without classic ST elevation. Treat as STEMI!
  • Posterior STEMI: ST depression V1–V3 with upright T → check V7–V9 (≥0.5mm elevation)
  • De Winter pattern: ST↓ at J-point with tall, peaked T-waves V1–V6 — proximal LAD occlusion
  • aVR STEMI: ST↑ in aVR ≥1mm with ST↓ elsewhere — Left main or proximal LAD (LMCA equivalent)
  • Wellens Syndrome: Biphasic or deeply inverted T V2–V3 in pain-free state — critical LAD stenosis
  • New LBBB with symptoms — treat as STEMI equivalent
  • New RBBB + LAFB with ischemic symptoms — bifascicular block, proximal LAD
ER ALGORITHM
Systematic Code STEMI Response Protocol
⚡ First 10 Minutes — Simultaneous Actions
🚨
Everything below happens SIMULTANEOUSLY, not sequentially. Delegate tasks to team members.
  • 12-lead ECG within 10 min of arrival — repeat if nondiagnostic but high suspicion. Activate Code STEMI if criteria met.
  • IV access × 2 (large bore, antecubital preferred) + cardiac monitoring + pulse oximetry
  • Aspirin 300mg PO (loading dose, non-enteric coated, chew) immediately — unless true allergy
  • MONA + Morphine → O₂ only if SpO₂ <90%, NTG if SBP >90 + no RV infarct, Morphine 2–4mg IV for refractory pain
  • Labs STAT: Troponin (hs-TnI/T), CK-MB, FBC, BMP, coagulation (PT/APTT), lipid panel, LFT, blood group & crossmatch
  • Call cardiologist / Cath Lab activation — do not wait for troponin results to activate!
  • Inferior STEMI: Right-sided ECG (V3R–V4R) to rule out RV infarct. Consider posterior leads (V7–V9).
  • Brief history: Symptom onset, prior MI/PCI/CABG, bleeding history, last meal (for emergent procedure), medications, allergies
  • CXR portable (do not delay PCI for CXR) — assess pulmonary edema, mediastinum width (exclude dissection)
🕐 Time-Based Action Timeline
T = 0 min — DOOR
Patient arrives / EMS handover
Triage IMMEDIATELY. Code STEMI activation. Nurse takes vitals, attaches monitor. Do not put in waiting room.
T = ≤10 min — 12-LEAD ECG
ECG Acquisition & Interpretation
Physician reads within 10 min. STEMI confirmed → ACTIVATE. If LBBB with symptoms → treat as STEMI equivalent. Consider Sgarbossa criteria.
T = 10–20 min — DUAL ANTIPLATELET
Antiplatelet Loading
Aspirin 300mg (if not given) PLUS P2Y12 inhibitor:
• Ticagrelor 180mg (preferred) OR
• Clopidogrel 600mg (if Ticagrelor unavailable/contraindicated)
• Prasugrel 60mg (if going to PCI, no prior stroke/TIA, age <75, wt >60kg)
T = 15–30 min — ANTICOAGULATION
Anticoagulant Choice
For PCI strategy: UFH 70–100 U/kg IV bolus (max 10,000 U) OR Bivalirudin (preferred if HIT risk)
For fibrinolysis: Enoxaparin or UFH per protocol (see Reperfusion tab)
T = ≤30 min — DECISION POINT
PCI vs Fibrinolysis Decision
If PCI available ≤120 min → primary PCI. If not → fibrinolysis within 30 min of door time (if no contraindications). See Reperfusion tab for full decision tree.
T = 30–60 min — TRANSFER / CATH LAB
Transport & Stabilization
Continuous monitoring during transport. Defibrillator immediately available. Treat arrhythmias. Manage hemodynamics (see Complications tab).
T = ≤90 min — BALLOON INFLATION (D2B)
Primary PCI — Culprit Vessel Recanalized
Wire crosses lesion → balloon/stent deployed. TIMI 3 flow restored. Post-PCI: Continue DAPT, anticoagulation, statin, ACEi. ICU/CCU admission.

🫀
Cardiogenic Shock triad: SBP <90 mmHg (or drop ≥30 from baseline) + signs of hypoperfusion (cold, oliguric, confused) + evidence of adequate filling pressure. Mortality 40–80% — emergent revascularization is the only proven therapy.

PCI vs Fibrinolysis — Decision Framework
STEMI CONFIRMED

⚡ Reperfusion Decision Algorithm
PCI-capable center available?
Expected D2B ≤120 min from FMC?
↙ YES                NO ↘
✅ PRIMARY PCI
  • D2B goal: ≤90 min
  • FMC-to-device: ≤120 min
  • Preferred over fibrinolysis in ALL eligible patients
  • No time limit if PCI available ≤120 min
  • Also preferred: cardiogenic shock, large territory, LBBB, contraindications to fibrinolysis
⚡ FIBRINOLYSIS
  • D2N goal: ≤30 min
  • FMC-to-needle: ≤30 min
  • Give if PCI delay >120 min AND onset <12h
  • Pharmacoinvasive strategy: PCI 3–24h after successful lysis
  • RESCUE PCI if fibrinolysis fails (<50% ST resolution at 60–90 min)

💊 Fibrinolytic Agents (Indonesia Context)
Streptokinase (SK)
1.5 million U IV in 100 mL NS over 60 min
Most widely available in Indonesia. Non-fibrin-specific. Antigenic — cannot re-use within 6 months to 2 years. Risk of allergic reaction and hypotension. TIMI 3 flow ~30%.
⚠ Monitor BP closely — hypotension is common. Slow infusion if BP drops.
Alteplase (tPA)
15mg IV bolus → 0.75 mg/kg over 30 min (max 50mg) → 0.5 mg/kg over 60 min (max 35mg). Total max: 100mg
Fibrin-specific. Better TIMI 3 flow (~54%). Higher intracranial hemorrhage risk. Can be re-used. Preferred for large anterior STEMI or high-risk patients if SK unavailable.
Tenecteplase (TNK-tPA)
Single IV bolus based on weight: <60kg=30mg | 60–70kg=35mg | 70–80kg=40mg | 80–90kg=45mg | >90kg=50mg
Fibrin-specific, single bolus (easiest to administer). Fibrinogen depletion minimal. Non-inferior to alteplase. Preferred in pre-hospital setting. May not be widely available in all hospitals.
✅
Post-fibrinolysis: Transfer to PCI center within 3–24h for coronary angiography (pharmacoinvasive strategy). Perform RESCUE PCI immediately if <50% ST resolution at 60–90 min post-lysis.
🔴 Special Situations
🫀 RV Infarct Management▼
🚨
AVOID: Nitrates, Diuretics, Morphine — all reduce preload in a preload-dependent RV!
  • Volume load NS 250–500 mL IV bolus carefully — if hypotensive
  • Maintain AV synchrony — pace if high-degree AV block (junctional rhythm common)
  • Dobutamine if RV failure persists after volume loading
  • Emergent PCI of RCA — restores RV function rapidly
  • Avoid excessive ventilatory pressures if intubated (increases RV afterload)
⚡ STEMI + Cardiogenic Shock▼
  • Emergent PCI regardless of time from onset — only proven survival benefit
  • Vasopressor: Norepinephrine first-line (MAP target ≥65 mmHg) — dopamine associated with higher arrhythmia
  • Inotrope: Dobutamine 2–20 mcg/kg/min if low CO without extreme hypotension
  • IABP: Consider if available (reduces afterload, improves coronary perfusion)
  • Intubation if respiratory failure — caution: peri-intubation hemodynamic collapse
  • Do NOT fibrinolyze cardiogenic shock if PCI available — PCI is superior
💔 STEMI in Cardiac Arrest (OHCA)▼
  • High-quality CPR, early defibrillation — standard ACLS
  • Post-ROSC ECG — if STEMI present → emergent PCI regardless of consciousness
  • Targeted Temperature Management (TTM) 32–36°C if comatose post-ROSC
  • Coronary angiography even if no ST elevation — 30–40% have culprit occlusion
  • Watch for post-cardiac arrest syndrome: myocardial dysfunction, systemic ischemia-reperfusion
MEDICATIONS
Acute & Post-STEMI Drug Protocol — Indonesia Context
💊 Acute Phase — DAPT & Anticoagulation
Aspirin (Aspilets)
Loading: 300mg PO (non-EC, chewed) | Maintenance: 75–100mg OD
Give immediately regardless of reperfusion strategy. Irreversible COX-1 inhibition. If truly allergic, use clopidogrel monotherapy.
Ticagrelor (Brilinta)
Loading: 180mg PO | Maintenance: 90mg BD (12h intervals)
P2Y12 inhibitor, reversible. Preferred over clopidogrel (PLATO trial). Onset faster, more potent. Not crushed/NG safe if peri-procedural. Duration: 12 months.
⚠ CI: Prior ICH, active pathological bleeding, severe hepatic impairment, strong CYP3A4 inhibitors. Side effect: Dyspnea (~15%) — usually self-limiting. Avoid in patients needing dialysis.
Clopidogrel (Plavix / generics)
Loading: 600mg PO (PCI) or 300mg (fibrinolysis) | Maintenance: 75mg OD
Most widely available in Indonesia. Prodrug — CYP2C19 metabolism (poor metabolizers have reduced efficacy). Use if Ticagrelor unavailable, contraindicated, or with OAC co-therapy.
⚠ CYP2C19 genetic polymorphism affects ~30% Asians — consider genotyping if available
Prasugrel (Effient)
Loading: 60mg PO | Maintenance: 10mg OD (5mg if <60kg or ≥75yr)
Faster, more potent than clopidogrel. PCI only (not for fibrinolysis). More consistent than clopidogrel, no CYP2C19 issue.
⚠ ABSOLUTE CI: Prior stroke/TIA | Relative CI: Age ≥75yr, weight <60kg (higher bleeding risk)
🩸 Anticoagulants
Unfractionated Heparin (UFH)
PCI: 70–100 U/kg IV bolus (max 10,000 U). Target ACT 250–350s (or 200–250s if GPI used). Fibrinolysis: 60 U/kg bolus (max 4000 U) then 12 U/kg/h infusion (max 1000 U/h)
Most available in Indonesia. Weight-based dosing. Reversible with protamine. Monitor aPTT.
Enoxaparin (Lovenox / Clexane)
With fibrinolysis: Age <75yr: 30mg IV bolus then 1mg/kg SC q12h (max 100mg first 2 doses). Age ≥75yr: NO bolus, 0.75mg/kg SC q12h. eGFR <30: 1mg/kg SC OD
Preferred for medical management (non-PCI). Continue up to 8 days or until PCI. Do not switch between heparins if going to PCI.
Fondaparinux
2.5mg SC OD (with initial UFH bolus for PCI to prevent catheter thrombosis)
Best safety profile for fibrinolysis strategy or conservative management. NOT recommended as sole anticoagulant during PCI (catheter thrombosis risk).
💊 Post-STEMI Essential Medications
High-Intensity Statin
Atorvastatin 40–80mg OD or Rosuvastatin 20–40mg OD — start in ER, continue long-term
Pleiotropic effects beyond LDL lowering. Start early regardless of baseline LDL. Target LDL <1.4 mmol/L (<55 mg/dL) in very high risk (post-ACS).
Beta-Blocker (BB)
Bisoprolol 1.25–10mg OD or Carvedilol 3.125–25mg BD or Metoprolol succinate 25–200mg OD
Start within 24h if hemodynamically stable (HR >60, SBP >100, no HF, no cardiogenic shock, no AV block). Long-term: reduces mortality, remodeling, arrhythmia.
⚠ Do NOT give IV beta-blockers routinely in acute phase. Oral preferred. Contraindicated: Acute decompensated HF, significant bradycardia, cardiogenic shock, active bronchospasm.
ACEi / ARB
Ramipril 2.5–10mg OD or Lisinopril 5–40mg OD | ARB (if ACEi intolerant): Valsartan 40–160mg BD
Start within 24h, especially if LVEF <40%, anterior STEMI, DM, HTN. Reduces ventricular remodeling, mortality. If LVEF <35% and symptoms → add MRA.
MRA (Aldosterone Antagonist)
Eplerenone 25–50mg OD (preferred) or Spironolactone 25–50mg OD
Indicated if LVEF ≤40% + symptomatic HF or DM. Start within 3–14 days post-MI. Avoid if K+ >5.0 or creatinine >220 (men) / >177 (women) µmol/L.

COMPLICATIONS
Recognition, Management & Prevention
⚡ Early Complications (<24–48h)
❤️ Ventricular Arrhythmias (VF/VT)▼
🚨
VF = most common cause of death in first hour post-STEMI. Defibrillate immediately.
ArrhythmiaTimingAction
Primary VF<48hDefibrillate → Amiodarone → Correct K+/Mg2+
Reperfusion VFMinutes post-PCIDefibrillate. Usually self-limiting. Good prognostic sign.
Accelerated Idioventricular Rhythm (AIVR)Post-reperfusionObserve. Usually benign. Do NOT suppress.
Late VT/VF (>48h)>48hAmiodarone, implantable defibrillator (ICD) if LVEF <35% at 90 days
🔑
Prevention: Correct K+ to 4.0–5.0 mEq/L and Mg2+ to 0.8–1.0 mmol/L. Beta-blockers reduce VF risk. Reperfusion is the best prophylaxis.

🩺 Acute Mitral Regurgitation▼
Cause: Papillary muscle rupture (usually posterior, RCA territory) — Day 2–7 post-MI
Presentation: Sudden acute pulmonary edema, new loud holosystolic murmur (may be soft if low EF), hemodynamic collapse
Diagnosis: Echo (flail leaflet, regurgitant jet), Swan-Ganz (large V-waves in PCWP)
Management: Intra-aortic balloon pump (reduces afterload) + urgent cardiac surgery (MV repair/replacement) ± vasodilators (NTG, nitroprusside if BP allows). Emergent PCI of culprit if not yet done.
🔴 Ventricular Septal Rupture (VSR)▼
Timing: Day 1–14, peak Day 3–5 (fibrinolytics: earlier)
Location: Anterior (LAD infarct) → apical VSR | Inferior (RCA) → basal VSR (worse prognosis)
Signs: New harsh systolic murmur at LLSB + thrill + acute hemodynamic deterioration
Diagnosis: Echo (L→R shunt), O2 step-up in RV on Swan-Ganz
Management: Stabilize with IABP + vasodilators → urgent surgical repair or percutaneous closure. Medical therapy alone: mortality >90%
